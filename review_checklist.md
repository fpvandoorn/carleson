# Review checklist

These are some of the points we need to consider when reviewing the full draft of the paper:

* Consistent US spelling (formalize/formalization, etc).
* Consistent capitalization in titles.
* Consistent capitalization when referring to results (Theorem a.b vs theorem a.b).
* Micro-typography (such as spacing after abbreviations like `e.g.`, `i.e.`, etc.; non-breaking spaces like Definition~1.2)
* References don't appear as subject or objects in sentences
* Superfluous modal verbs (such as can, would, should, will)
* Use \mathlib macro instead of mathlib or Mathlib.
* Consistent hyphenation (real-valued, non-zero, etc).
* Use \emph when introducing definitions.
* Check punctuation, especially in displayed formulas.
* Hyphenation between author names (--).
* Spell-check everything (similar to the above, but lower key)
